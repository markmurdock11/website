The thing they don’t tell you about taking a job in tech or a remote job or really any job in person is that if it goes really well or really bad, because relationships do best when there are peaks or valleys to celebrate or denigrate, the relationships you find along the way will be glorious and will take you to new corners of the world and into conversations that you never would have imagined, meeting people and seeing things that are the exact things that make life worth living.

In Croatia, there is a town called Bjelovar and it is the hometown of Marko, my friend, my good friend, a new dad, husband to the pants-wearing Spela, son of the Jurina family, an older brother with intensity. Native to Croatia and now in Slovenia, Marko can always be found with a cigarette just being rolled, you never see him smoke it because he smokes as he lives and breathes. You only notice the rolling, the light break in the conversation that paces the day as he opens up his pack of tobacco and rips open a new filter, his eyes alit and mind still on whatever conspiracy theory topic is being thrown around, fingers on autopilot, rolling and rolling and rolling.

There are so many moments on this trip that are deeply personal but so universal that I just want to get them out of my head and into the world. The way that Marko and Spela, the two lovers who are so perfectly matched in giving each other shit but have been through so much, held hands in the yellow and pink light of the Petar Graso concert, singing words about love and dancing and staring at each other, forgetting the other people around them, turning it into a moment for two.
